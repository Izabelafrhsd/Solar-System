Our Solar System

Our solar system features eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Please note that Pluto is NOT considered a planet.

The Sun is also NOT a planet, it is a type of star.

Mercury

The first planet in the solar system is Mercury.

Mercury is the smallest planet out of the eight.

Its width, compared to the Earth, is 1/3 of its size.

Additionally, Mercury is the closest to the Sun, despite it being a lot of miles away.

Did You Know?: Mercury is about 57 MILLION miles away!

Venus

The second planet in the solar system is Venus.

Venus is the sixth largest planet out of the eight

Its width, compared to the Earth, is smaller. However, they are almost similar in size.

Venus is the second closest to the Sun, despite its huge distance.

Did You Know?:Venus is known to be Earth's twin!

Earth

The fifth largest planet in the solar system is Earth.

Earth is the fifth largest planet out of the eight as well.

Its width, is equatorial diameter of 7.926 miles

Did You Knows?:Mostly covered in water.

Mars

The third in the solar system is Mars.

Mars is the seventh largest planet out of the eight.

Its width, is an equatorial diameter of 4,221 miles.

Mars is the third closest to the Sun, even though it is far away.

Did You Know?:Mars is also known as The Red Planet.

Jupiter

The fourth in the solar system is Jupiter.

Jupiter is the LARGEST planet out of the eight.

Its width, is...HUGE!

Jupiter is the fourth closest to the Sun.

Did You Know?: Jupiter is 11 TIMES larger than Earth!
